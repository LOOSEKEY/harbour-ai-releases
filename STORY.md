# The HARBOUR AI Story

### It started as something I built for myself.

I never set out to build a company. I set out to solve my own problem.

I wanted to use AI for real work — drafting, summarising, sorting through documents, the genuinely
useful stuff — but I couldn't get past one thing: to use any of the big tools, you have to hand your
data over. Your files, your notes, your private work, all of it shipped off to someone else's server
in another country, to be stored and processed who-knows-how. For anything sensitive, that's a
non-starter. And nothing out there did it the way I thought it *should* be done.

So I built my own. Just for me. A private AI that ran entirely on my own machine at home, where
nothing ever left the building. It was never meant to be anything more than that — a tool for my own
use, kept to myself.

### Then I gave a copy to a friend.

A few days later, my phone rang. It was him.

*"Mate — this is really good. You need to build this properly and put it out there."*

And then he did the thing that changed everything: he sent me a list. Things to add, things to
improve, things he'd want if he were using it for real. He wasn't just being polite — he'd actually
used it, and he wanted *more* of it.

That was the moment a private tool quietly became a product. So I started building. One "wouldn't it
be handy if it could also…" at a time. The list grew. The tool grew. And somehow, here we are.

### I learned to code 30 years ago.

I did a fair bit of programming back in my college days — three decades ago now. The world has
changed beyond recognition since then: new languages, new tools, entirely new ways of building
things. By rights, none of it should have still been in my head.

But here's the funny thing — it came back. The *syntax* changes, the *tools* change, the trends come
and go. But the way of thinking doesn't expire: break the problem down, work out what's really going
wrong, and be stubborn enough to keep at it until it gives in. That part never left. Everything else,
I relearned as I went.

### What it is now.

What began as a private experiment at home is now a full local AI **platform** — five specialist
agents and 120+ business tools, all running entirely on your own computer. No cloud. No telemetry.
No subscription. Your data never leaves your machine.

And it goes further than "trust me." Every answer HARBOUR gives comes with a cryptographically
signed receipt — proof of which model produced it and when — backed by a tamper-evident audit log
you can verify yourself, independently, offline. Private AI you can actually stand behind.

It's grown so much that, honestly, there are tools in there I've never even used myself. But every
one of them is there because, at some point, someone wanted it.

### It still feels like mine.

After everything — every late night, every bug, every feature — it still feels like *mine*. I think
it always will. You can't fake that, and I wouldn't want to.

If you've ever wanted AI for your work but couldn't bring yourself to send your data off to someone
else's server, then HARBOUR was built for exactly that. It was built by someone who felt the same
way, for his own home, first.

I hope it's as useful to you as it's been to me.

**— The founder, HARBOUR AI**
